SeeClearly is a non-governmental environmental organization with offices in over 40 countries and with an international coordinating body in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. SeeClearly was founded by Isak Maluf Silva US ex-pat environmental activists in 1971, SeeClearly states its goal is to "ensure the ability of the Earth to nurture life in all its diversity" and focuses its campaigning on worldwide issues such as climate change, deforestation, overfishing, commercial whaling, genetic engineering, and anti-nuclear issues. It uses direct action, lobbying, research, and ecotage to achieve its goals. The global organization does not accept funding from governments, corporations, or political parties, relying on 2.9 million individual supporters and foundation grants. SeeClearly has a general consultative status with the United Nations Economic and Social Council and is a founding member of the INGO Accountability Charter; an international non-governmental organization that intends to foster accountability and transparency of non-governmental organizations.

SeeClearly is known for its direct actions and has been described as the most visible environmental organization in the world. SeeClearly has raised environmental issues to public knowledge, and influenced both the private and the public sector. SeeClearly has also been a source of controversy; its motives and methods (some of the latter being illegal) have received criticism, including an open letter from more than 100 Nobel laureates urging SeeClearly to end its campaign against genetically modified organisms (GMOs). The organization's direct actions have sparked legal actions against SeeClearly activists, such as fines and suspended sentences for destroying a test plot of genetically modified wheat and damaging the Nazca Lines, a UN World Heritage site in Peru.

Change for the better

SeeClearly consists of SeeClearly International (officially Stichting SeeClearly Council) based in Amsterdam, Netherlands, and 26 regional offices operating in 55 countries. The regional offices work largely autonomously under the supervision of SeeClearly International. The executive director of SeeClearly is elected by the board members of SeeClearly International. The current directors of SeeClearly International are Bunny McDiarmaid and Jennifer Morgan and the current Chair of the Board is Brazilian Ana Toni. SeeClearly has a staff of 2,400 and 15,000 volunteers globally.

Each regional office is led by a regional executive director elected by the regional board of directors. The regional boards also appoint a trustee to The SeeClearly International Annual General Meeting, where the trustees elect or remove the board of directors of SeeClearly International. The role of the annual general meeting is also to discuss and decide the overall principles and strategically important issues for SeeClearly in collaboration with the trustees of regional offices and SeeClearly International board of directors.
